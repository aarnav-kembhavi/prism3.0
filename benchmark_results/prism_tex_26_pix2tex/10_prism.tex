\subsection*{0.1.2Gravitino equation}


The analysis of the gravitino equation oA = 0 proceeds in exactly the same way as for the
Lorentzian case studied in . The procedure gives rise to a first-order equation for the warp
factor f and an algebraic constraint. To avoid excessive overlap with that paper, we simply
cite the result,.


\begin{equation}
f^{\prime}=2(G_{0}S_{0}+G_{3}S_{3})\quad\qquad e^{-2f}=4(G_{0}S_{0}+G_{3}S_{3})^{2}-4(S_{0}^{2}+S_{3}^{2})\quad\qquad\qquad(5)
\end{equation}
