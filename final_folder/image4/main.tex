\subsection*{C. REPRODUCIBILITY}


To guarantee reproducibility, random seeds are
fixed at initialization for PyTorch, NumPy, and
CUDA backends. Model weights are initialized
using Py Torch default schemes, including Kaiming
initialization for convolutional and linear layers.
During training, checkpoints store model state dictionaries at epochs that achieve the highest validation accuracy.


\subsection*{D. EVALUATION PROTOCOL}


After training concludes, the checkpoint with the
best validation performance is reloaded for final
evaluation on the held-out test set. We report overall accuracy (the percentage of correctly classified
samples) and the macro-averaged F1-score (the
harmonic mean of precision and recall, averaged
over all activity classes). Confusion matrices are
generated to illustrate per-class error distributions.

\subsection*{B. THE ATTENTION PARADOX}


A noteworthy finding is that introducing temporal
attention to the convolutional-LSTM baseline reduces accuracy by 0.44 percentage points—-a result
opposite to trends observed in natural language
processing, where attention often yields 2-5\% improvements. This outcome challenges the common
expectation that attention mechanisms should always enhance model performance by emphasizing
the most informative time segments. Several factors may contribute to this phenomenon:

\begin{itemize}
\item 1) The baseline LSTM may already capture
enough temporal context through its 1hidden statedynamics, leavinglittle benefit for
added attention.

\item 2) The additional attention parameters could
 promote overfitting given the limited training
data of 7.352samples.

\item 3)Key activitycuesmaybe spreadacross entire
sequences rather than concentrated at specific
timesteps.

\item 4)The scoring function used in the attention
module may  not align well vwith the statistical
nature of inertialsensorssignals.

\end{itemize}

Interestingly, aattention does improve results
when  combined  with bidirectional recurrence
(Model1 C:  93.38\%6 vs. unidirectional baseline:
93.08\%). This aligns with prior ensemble-based
research [24], suggesting that richer per-timestep
representations from bidirectional processing provide stronger inputs for selective attention mechanisms.
